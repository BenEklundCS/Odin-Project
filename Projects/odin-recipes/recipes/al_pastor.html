Al Pastor

In this recipe, you will learn how to make Al Pastor tacos!

The recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of boneless pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried guajillo chilies, seeds removed
  • 2 dried ancho chilies, seeds removed
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons achiote paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade:

    • Heat a dry skillet over medium heat and toast the dried chilies for about 1-2 minutes until they become fragrant.
    • Place the toasted chilies in a bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 15-20 minutes until they soften.
    • Drain the chilies and place them in a blender or food processor.
    • Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, white vinegar, achiote paste, ground cumin, dried oregano, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper to the blender.
    • Blend until you have a smooth paste. You may need to add a little water if it's too thick.

  2. Marinate the Pork:

    • Place the thinly sliced pork in a large bowl.
    • Pour the marinade over the pork and toss to coat the meat thoroughly.
    • Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results, allowing the flavors to meld.

  3. Cook the Al Pastor:

    • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
    • Thread the marinated pork slices onto skewers or onto a rotisserie spit.
    • Grill the pork for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until it's cooked through and has a nice char.
    • While grilling, you can also place the pineapple slices on the grill for a minute or two on each side until they have grill marks.

  4. Assemble the Tacos:

    • Warm the corn tortillas either on the grill or in a skillet.
    • To assemble each taco, place some of the grilled Al Pastor pork on a tortilla.
    • Top with a slice of grilled pineapple, thinly sliced red onion, chopped fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.
    • Add your favorite salsa to taste.

Serve:

Serve your delicious Al Pastor tacos immediately with extra lime wedges and salsa on the side.
